VA Hiring SOP

Role: Founder
When: After 3-5 clients (remove yourself from delivery)
Source: Kai Stone Systems VA Hiring SOP
Last Updated: Feb 22, 2026

When to Hire

Don't hire before you have clients.

The point of hiring a VA is to remove yourself from service delivery. You should:

  1. Have done a few projects yourself

  2. Know how everything works

  3. Have SOPs documented


Start with project-based work to keep costs low.

Hiring Process Overview

  1. Post job on platforms
  2. Invite many applicants
  3. Require Loom video (no video = no interview)
  4. First-round interviews
  5. Second interview if needed
  6. Assign test project
  7. Hire

Interview Process

What to Look For

Immediate disqualifiers:
  • Shows up late
  • Internet issues during call
  • Unpresentable (messy background, disheveled)
  • Can't communicate clearly in English
Green flags:
  • On time
  • Clean background
  • Professional demeanor
  • Asks good questions
  • GHL or similar platform experience

Questions to Ask

  1. What are you expecting to earn?
  2. Do you have any experience with GHL?
  3. What times are you available to work?
  4. Do you already have a job?
  5. Where have you worked in the past?
  6. How do you prefer to receive payment? (PayPal, Wise, Remitly)
  7. Are you open to project-based work initially?

Interview Style

  • Keep it relaxed and conversational
  • You're evaluating fit, not grilling them
  • Let them talk — their communication style matters
  • Take notes

Test Project

Before full hire, assign a small paid project:

Example:
  • Build one client website from onboarding form
  • Pay $20-50 for the project
  • Evaluate quality, speed, and communication
What to evaluate:
  • Did they follow instructions?
  • Did they ask clarifying questions?
  • Was the quality acceptable?
  • Did they meet the deadline?
  • Were they responsive?

Onboarding the VA

Day 1-3: Orientation

  1. Share all SOPs (give portal access)
  2. Walk through one project together on Zoom
  3. Give access to necessary tools:
- GHL (appropriate permissions) - Communication (Slack/WhatsApp) - Task management (if using)

Week 1: Supervised Projects

  1. Assign 2-3 projects
  2. Review output together
  3. Give detailed feedback
  4. Answer all questions

Week 2+: Independence

  1. Assign projects with deadlines
  2. Check in daily initially, then every few days
  3. Spot-check quality
  4. Provide ongoing feedback

Communication Setup

Recommended: WhatsApp or Slack

  • Daily check-ins initially
  • Async updates (they share progress, you review)
  • Clear escalation path for questions

Meeting Cadence

  • Week 1: Daily 15-min sync
  • Week 2-4: Every other day
  • Month 2+: Weekly 30-min call

Common Issues & Fixes

IssueFix
Slow responsesSet clear response time expectations (30 min during work hours)
Quality droppingMore frequent check-ins, clearer feedback
Missed deadlinesBreak projects into smaller chunks with checkpoints
Communication gapsRequire daily end-of-day summary

When to Let Go

Fire quickly if:

  • Consistent missed deadlines

  • Quality doesn't improve after feedback

  • Dishonest or unreliable

  • Unprofessional with clients


Process:
  1. Give clear warning with specific issues

  2. Set improvement deadline (1 week)

  3. If no improvement, end relationship

  4. Pay what's owed, keep it professional

Checklist: First VA Hire

  • Post on 1-2 platforms
  • Collect video applications
  • Interview top 5 candidates
  • Assign test project to top 2
  • Select winner
  • Set up payment method
  • Share SOP portal access
  • Do first project together on Zoom
  • Set communication cadence
  • Weekly check-ins for first month
