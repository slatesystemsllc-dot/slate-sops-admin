3. HANDLING "I HAVEN'T WATCHED"

A: Ready to Watch Now

"No worries at all. It's only about 5 minutes. You have a sec to watch it real quick? I can wait." [If yes] "Perfect. I just sent it to you again via text. Let me know when you've got it pulled up." [MUTE YOURSELF. Wait 5-6 minutes.] [When done] "Cool, appreciate you taking the time. So that's pretty much exactly what we do. What questions do you have?"

B: They're Driving

"No worries man, safety first. I'll shoot you the link right now, watch it when you get where you're going, then just text me 'ready' and I'll call you right back. Sound good?"

C: Too Busy

"I hear you, but honestly the video does a way better job than I can in 2 minutes. It shows real client results. Tell you what - it's only 5 minutes. Watch it tonight and let's reschedule for tomorrow. What time works?"

D: Refuses

"We've found guys who watch first have way better calls. Can you commit to watching before we talk again?" [If still no] "No worries man, sounds like timing might not be right. Feel free to book another call when you've got a few minutes."

Don't waste time on people who won't invest 5 minutes.

4. OBJECTION HANDLING

"How many leads will I get?"

"I'm gonna be straight - I can't tell you Susan sees your reviews and gives you a $20K job. I can't prove that correlation. What I know is contractors with professional websites, tons of reviews, and fast follow-up are winning. We give you the systems. You think it's worth $297 or you don't."

"I've been burned before"

"I hear that a lot. Most marketing companies overpromise. I'm not promising leads. I'm giving you systems. Website, reviews, missed call follow-up. You saw it. $297, no contracts, cancel anytime. What other questions?"

"That's expensive"

"What's your average job ticket? $5K? $10K? If these systems help you land ONE extra job this year, you're 3-4x'ing what you paid us. One job. $297 is about what you'd spend on crew lunch twice."

"Let me talk to my wife"

"Totally get it. When will you have that conversation? Cool, let's get something on the calendar for [day after]. That way I can answer their questions too."
