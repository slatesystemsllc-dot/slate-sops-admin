Show Rate Optimization SOP

Role: Closer / Sales
Goal: Increase show rate from ~40% to 60%+
Source: Kai Stone Systems Show Rate Secrets
Last Updated: Feb 22, 2026

Why This Matters

More shows = more conversations = more closes.

Math: If you book 20 calls/week:
  • 40% show rate = 8 conversations
  • 60% show rate = 12 conversations
  • That's 50% more opportunities to close
Before fixing anything else in sales, fix show rate.

3 Fast Wins (Do These First)

1. Tighten Booking Window

Problem: Prospects who book a week out forget, lose interest, or get busy. Fix: Cap scheduling to 48-72 hours maximum.

In your calendar settings:

  • Set "Days in advance" to 2-3 days max

  • Remove availability beyond 72 hours

  • The closer the booking to now, the higher the show


2. Call Every Appointment Immediately + Morning Of

When they book:
  1. Call them within 5 minutes
  2. Confirm the time
  3. Try to pull the meeting forward if slots available
  4. Build rapport (they're more likely to show for someone they've talked to)
Morning of the call:
  1. Call them to confirm
  2. "Hey, just confirming we're still on for [time] today"
  3. If voicemail, text them
This single habit dramatically increases shows.

3. Upgrade Your Thank You Page

After they book, they land on your thank you page. Add:

  1. Short video (30-60 seconds)
- "Hey, excited to chat! Here's what we'll cover..." - Sets expectations, builds anticipation
  1. Big "Add to Calendar" button
- Google Calendar + iCal options - Anchors them to the commitment
  1. Commitment checkbox (optional but powerful)
- "Is there any reason besides the world ending you wouldn't show up?" - Psychological commitment device

Expanded Show Rate Levers

Lever 1: Expand Calendar Availability

Problem: Leads book inconvenient times because nothing else was available. Fix:
  • Minimum 6-8 slots per day
  • Shorten appointment duration if needed (45 min → 30 min)
  • Offer varied times:
  • Early morning (7-9 AM) for 9-5 employees
  • Lunch (12-1 PM)
  • Evening (6-8 PM)
  • Mid-morning (10-11 AM) for entrepreneurs
Match availability to your market.

Lever 2: Multi-Touch Confirmation Sequence

Call schedule:
  1. Immediately after booking
  2. 60 minutes later if no answer
  3. 24 hours before
  4. Morning of
  5. 1 hour before
  6. 10 minutes before
Text sequence:
  • Booking confirmation (immediate)
  • 24-hour reminder
  • 1-hour reminder with meeting link

Lever 3: Reframe the Call Value

Don't say:
  • "Demo"
  • "Quick chat"
  • "Let's talk"
Do say:
  • "Strategy session"
  • "I'll Google your business and show you quick wins"
  • "I'll pull up your competitors and show you what they're doing"
Give them a reason to show up beyond being sold.

Lever 4: Speed to Lead

If you have 10+ leads/day:
  • Call leads within 30 seconds of form submission
  • 4,000% increase in close rate vs waiting even 5 minutes
  • First to call usually wins
Set up instant notification:
  • Slack/SMS alert on new lead
  • Call immediately, every time

Lever 5: Double Booking Strategy

When you have excess leads:
  1. Book 2 people in the same slot
  2. Call both 10-15 minutes before
  3. Most qualified/ready → confirm for video call
  4. Less qualified → try to close on phone or rebook
Why this works:
  • You never have empty slots
  • Maximizes your calendar efficiency
  • Not unethical — staying broke is

Platform: Google Meet > Zoom

  • Contractors are more familiar with Google
  • One less friction point
  • No "download Zoom" barrier
  • If they can't do video, close on phone

Checklist: New Appointment Flow

When someone books:

  • Call them within 5 minutes
  • Try to pull meeting forward if earlier slot available
  • If no answer, text: "Hey [Name], just confirming our call at [time]. Looking forward to it!"
  • Ensure they're added to reminder sequence
  • Check if they watched VSL (if not, send reminder)
Day of:
  • Morning call to confirm
  • 1-hour reminder (call or text)
  • 10-minute reminder with link
  • If they don't show in 5 min, call them
