Cancellation Save SOP

Role: Closer / Founder
When: Client requests to cancel
Goal: Retain client or learn why they're leaving
Last Updated: Feb 22, 2026

Mindset

Every cancellation request is:

  1. An opportunity to fix a problem

  2. A chance to learn what's not working

  3. A potential save if handled right


Never process a cancellation without a conversation.

The Process

Step 1: Call Them (Don't Text/Email)

When you get a cancellation request:

  1. Call them within 2 hours

  2. Don't process the cancellation yet

  3. If no answer, try 2 more times same day


Why call: Text makes it easy to dismiss. Phone creates obligation and lets you address concerns.

Step 2: Opening (No Pressure)

"Hey [Name], it's [Your Name] from Slate Systems. I got your message about canceling. No pressure at all — I just wanted to understand what's going on. Is everything okay?"
Key: Come from a place of service, not desperation.

Step 3: Listen & Understand

Let them talk. Take notes on:

  • Specific complaints

  • What's not working

  • External factors (business slow, personal issues)

  • Competitor mentions


Don't interrupt or defend. Just listen.

Step 4: Respond Based on Reason

ReasonResponse
"Not getting leads""I hear you. Can we hop on a quick call and look at your Google together? Sometimes small tweaks — like updating your GMB description or getting a few more reviews — can make a big difference. Would you give me 15 minutes to see if we can turn it around?"
"Can't afford it right now""I totally understand. What if we paused your account for a month or two instead of canceling? That way your website stays up and you can pick back up when things improve."
"Not using it / Too busy""Makes sense — you're running a business. What if I showed you just the review piece? It takes 30 seconds to send a review request. That alone can bring in more jobs. Can I walk you through it real quick?"
"Going with someone else""Respect that. Mind if I ask what they're offering? Not trying to change your mind, just want to understand what we could've done better."
"Bad experience / Frustrated""I'm really sorry to hear that. Can you tell me more about what happened? I want to make it right if I can."
"Business is closing / Retiring"Accept gracefully. Ask for referrals.

Step 5: Make an Offer (If Appropriate)

Based on the conversation:

For price concerns:
  • Pause account (no charge, keep site up)
  • One free month to "try one more time"
  • Downgrade to website-only ($97/mo)
For usage concerns:
  • Offer 15-minute training call
  • Send Loom walkthrough of specific feature
  • Simplify to just one feature (reviews)
For results concerns:
  • Offer audit call to look at their setup
  • Make specific improvements
  • Set 30-day check-in

Step 6: If They Still Want to Cancel

"I totally respect that. I'll process the cancellation. Before I do — would you mind if I asked one more question? What would we have needed to do differently to keep you?"

Take the feedback. Thank them. Don't burn the bridge.

"Thanks for being a client. If things change down the road, we'd love to have you back."

Authority limits:

  • One free month: Can do immediately
  • Downgrade: Can do immediately
  • Refunds >$100: Escalate to Founder

After the Call

If saved:
  • Add tag: save-attempt-won
  • Note what offer/promise was made
  • Set reminder to follow up in 14 days
  • Deliver on whatever you promised
If canceled:
  • Process cancellation in GHL
  • Add tag: churned
  • Note reason in contact record
  • Add to "win-back" sequence (90 days)

Cancellation Reasons Tracking

Log every cancellation:

Red Flags (Can't Save)

Some cancellations aren't worth fighting:

  • Business closing / retiring
  • Competitor poached with impossible offer
  • Completely unresponsive (ghosting)
  • Hostile / threatening
Let these go gracefully.

Win-Back Sequence

For churned clients, after 90 days:

"Hey [Name]! It's [Your Name] from Slate Systems. Hope you're doing well. Just checking in — things have been going great on our end and we've added some new features. If you ever want to pick back up, just let me know. No pressure!"

Some clients return when their situation changes.

Metrics
