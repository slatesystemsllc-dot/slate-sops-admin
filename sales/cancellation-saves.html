Mindset

Every cancellation request is:

  1. An opportunity to fix a problem
  2. A chance to learn what's not working
  3. A potential save if handled right
Never process a cancellation without a conversation.

The Process

Step 1: Call Them (Don't Text/Email)

When you get a cancellation request:

  1. Call them within 2 hours
  2. Don't process the cancellation yet
  3. If no answer, try 2 more times same day
Text makes it easy to dismiss. Phone creates obligation and lets you address concerns.

Step 2: Opening (No Pressure)

"Hey [Name], it's [Your Name] from Slate Systems. I got your message about canceling. No pressure at all ‚Äî I just wanted to understand what's going on. Is everything okay?"

Key: Come from a place of service, not desperation.

Step 3: Listen & Understand

Let them talk. Take notes on:

  • Specific complaints
  • What's not working
  • External factors (business slow, personal issues)
  • Competitor mentions
Don't interrupt or defend. Just listen.

Response by Reason

ReasonResponse
"Not getting leads" "Can we hop on a quick call and look at your Google together? Sometimes small tweaks can make a big difference. Would you give me 15 minutes?"
"Can't afford it" "What if we paused your account for a month or two instead of canceling? Your website stays up and you can pick back up when things improve."
"Not using it / Too busy" "What if I showed you just the review piece? It takes 30 seconds. That alone can bring in more jobs. Can I walk you through it?"
"Going with someone else" "Respect that. Mind if I ask what they're offering? Just want to understand what we could've done better."
"Bad experience" "I'm really sorry to hear that. Can you tell me more about what happened? I want to make it right if I can."
"Business closing" Accept gracefully. Ask for referrals.

After the Call

If Saved:

  • Add tag: save-attempt-won
  • Note what offer/promise was made
  • Set reminder to follow up in 14 days
  • Deliver on whatever you promised

If Canceled:

  • Process cancellation in GHL
  • Add tag: churned
  • Note reason in contact record
  • Add to "win-back" sequence (90 days)

Red Flags (Can't Save)

Some cancellations aren't worth fighting:

  • Business closing / retiring
  • Competitor poached with impossible offer
  • Completely unresponsive (ghosting)
  • Hostile / threatening

Let these go gracefully.

Win-Back Sequence

For churned clients, after 90 days:

"Hey [Name]! It's [Your Name] from Slate Systems. Hope you're doing well. Just checking in ‚Äî things have been going great on our end and we've added some new features. If you ever want to pick back up, just let me know. No pressure!"

Some clients return when their situation changes.

Metrics to Track:

  • Total cancellation requests
  • Saves vs churned
  • Save rate % (goal: 30%+)
  • Top cancellation reasons
