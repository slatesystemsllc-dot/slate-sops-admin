KEY PRINCIPLES

#

1. ASSUME THE CLOSE

  • Don't ask permission, state what will happen
  • "We'll just have you fill out the onboarding form..."
  • "Which company do you want us to work on?" (not "if")

2. BE POLARIZING

Say things they've NEVER heard from a salesperson:

  • "We're gonna try to sell you more shit later once you're happy."
  • "If you don't have a website, that's best case scenario."
  • "Contracts? Those are just used to scare you into not canceling."

3. SAME SIDE OF THE TABLE

  • Feel like you're walking a friend through something
  • Casual, cool, calm, collected

4. MAKE THEM PROBLEM AWARE

  • Point out issues casually (not salesy)
  • Give them "lightbulb moments"

5. CARD ALWAYS LAST

  • Get name, address, business info first
  • By the time you ask for card, they're committed

OBJECTION HANDLING

#

Legend: HIGH = 10+ calls, MED = 5-9 calls, LOW = 1-4 calls

"I don't have a website" HIGH
"That's actually the best case scenario for us. Otherwise I have to convince you your website's shit. If you don't have anything, we just set it all up fresh."
Reframes weakness as advantage. Polarizing.
"Do you have contracts?" HIGH
"No, we don't do contracts. Contracts are just used to lock you in. We'd rather earn your business every month. If it's not working for you, you can cancel anytime ‚Äî no hassle."
Confident tone reframes concern.
"I've been burned by 3 other companies" HIGH
"Look, I'm not going to promise you the world. Here's exactly what we do [point to video]. You either think it's worth $297 or you don't. I can't guarantee Susan gives you $50K. What I can guarantee is you'll have the systems in place."
Honest, no false promises = different.
"How many leads will I get?" HIGH
"I cannot prove that Susan saw your reviews and gave you a $15K job. What I know is guys with professional websites, tons of reviews, and fast follow-up are the ones winning. Does that make sense?"
Realistic expectations, logical.
"It seems expensive" MED
"What's your average job? $10K-$20K? If you get ONE job in a YEAR, you're 3-6x'ing what you pay us. It's $297/month - one job pays for 3 years."
Simple ROI math.
"I need to think about it" MED
"For sure. What specifically do you need to think about? Maybe I can clarify. ... Look, you either think this is worth $297 or you don't. What you saw is exactly what you get."
"Let me talk to my partner/wife" MED
"Totally get it. When will you have that conversation? Let's get something on the calendar so I can answer their questions too."
Note: Wife/partner objection is hardest.
"Do you do lead generation?" HIGH
"I hate marketing as lead gen. You're paying $297 for pre-built SYSTEMS. Leads come as a byproduct. If you want direct lead gen, that's paid ads at $1,500-3,000/month. We don't do that."
"I can do this myself" LOW
"You absolutely can. Same way I could watch YouTube and fix my own roof. But I'm gonna call a roofer because that's what he does every day. You should be out doing jobs, not messing with websites."
