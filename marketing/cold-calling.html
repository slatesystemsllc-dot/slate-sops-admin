Cold Calling SOP

Role: Sales / Closer / Setter
When: Lead generation phase, before paid ads
Source: Kai Stone Systems Cold Call Master Class
Last Updated: Feb 22, 2026

Why Cold Calling

Cold calling is a foundational skill that:

  • Builds sales confidence

  • Provides one-to-one feedback

  • Works without ad spend

  • Teaches objection handling


Do 500+ cold calls before moving to other methods. The skill transfers to everything else.

Finding Prospects

Method 1: Google Maps

  1. Open Google Maps
  2. Search: "[niche] [city]" (e.g., "plumber San Diego")
  3. Look for businesses WITHOUT websites or with poor websites
  4. Note their phone number and name

Method 2: Google Search

  1. Search: "[niche] near me"
  2. Look at the bottom of page 1 or page 2
  3. These businesses need help

What to Look For

Good prospects:
  • No website or outdated website
  • Few Google reviews (<50)
  • Local service business
  • Answers their phone
Skip:
  • Big franchises
  • Already has professional presence
  • Not answering after 2 tries

The Script

Opening (Build Local Trust)

"Hey, is this [their name / business name]?"
If yes:
"Hey what's up [Name]! My mom's friend Julie Becker referred me to you guys. She said you do really high quality work, the [niche] right?"
Why this works:
  • Name drop creates familiarity
  • "Mom's friend" = non-threatening
  • They'll question Julie for a second, then move on
If they ask who Julie is:
"She's a friend of my mom's, I've never met her personally. But she mentioned you guys when I told her what I do."

Transition to Offer

"Yeah, so she told me to give you guys a call. She said you guys don't have a website or anything like that?"
They'll either:
  • Confirm they don't have one → Continue
  • Say they do → "But you don't have [other service]?"
"Ok cool. Well I don't want to waste your time. I just graduated college and I started a little [your service] business. I've done [service] for a while but thought I'd finally make a business out of it."
"Right now I'm doing [service] super cheap with the hopes I might be able to get a testimonial from you. You know, saying I did a good job, was fast, you saw results. Do you mind if I build something out for you to take a look at?"

If They Ask Price

"So I cover the cost of the monthly hosting and the domain. I'm only charging [entry price] a month. And if you don't like it, I'll refund you no questions asked."

Objection Handling

"I'm too busy right now"

"Totally get it, you're running a business. What if I sent over a quick video showing what I'd build for you? You can watch it whenever. If you like it, we can chat. If not, no worries."

"We already have a website"

"Oh nice! Can I pull it up real quick? [Look at it] You know, one thing I noticed is [specific issue - not mobile friendly, no reviews, slow load time]. Would you be open to me showing you how that's hurting you?"

"We're not interested"

"No problem at all. Mind if I ask what you're currently doing for [leads/reviews/etc]? Just curious."

"How did you get my number?"

"My mom's friend Julie Becker mentioned you. She said you did good work."

"Call me back later"

"For sure. When's a good time? I'll put it in my calendar right now."
Actually call them back. Most people don't, so you stand out.

After the Call

If interested:

  1. Send case study video via text
  2. Send calendar booking link
  3. Follow up in 24 hours if no booking

If not interested now:

  1. Note reason in CRM
  2. Follow up in 30 days
  3. Add to long-term nurture

If no answer:

  1. Leave voicemail
  2. Send text: "Hey, just tried calling about your Google Business page. Mind giving me a call back?"
  3. Try again tomorrow

Voicemail Script

"Hey [Name], this is [Your Name]. Julie Becker told me to give you a call about your business. I have a quick question for you. Give me a call back at [your number]. Talk soon!"

Keep it under 15 seconds. Create curiosity.

Text Follow-Up

After voicemail:

"Hey [Name], just tried calling. Julie Becker mentioned you might need some help with your online presence. Mind if I send you a quick video?"

Best Practices

Do:

  • Smile while talking (they can hear it)
  • Stand up or walk around
  • Have energy
  • Keep it conversational
  • Actually call them back when you say you will

Don't:

  • Sound like you're reading a script
  • Talk too fast
  • Get defensive at objections
  • Give up after one "no"
  • Oversell on the first call

Numbers Game

Expect:

  • 50% no answer

  • 30% not interested

  • 15% maybe/follow-up

  • 5% interested now


To get 5 interested leads: Make 100 calls.

Daily Targets

Minimum: 500 total calls before moving to other methods.
